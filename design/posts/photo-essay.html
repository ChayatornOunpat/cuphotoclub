Night Markets

Six frames from Bangkok's after-dark bazaars — where light is scarce, colour is loud, and every stall tells a story.

Pim W. February 2025 6 photographs

There is a particular quality of light at a night market that no studio can replicate. It is the light of bare bulbs, fluorescent tubes, and the occasional streetlamp — inconsistent, chaotic, and alive. These are photographs from four different markets across Bangkok, shot over the course of a single month.

01 · A vendor arranging fruit, Yaowarat Road, 8:42 PM

The first rule of photographing night markets: accept that you will never have enough light. The second rule: stop worrying about it. At ISO 3200, the grain becomes part of the texture — it looks the way the market feels. Crisp and sharp is for studios. Night photography is about what you can almost see.

02–03 · Portrait of a grill master, and the queue that followed

I spent three evenings at the same seafood stall in Yaowarat before the owner stopped noticing my camera. That is when the real photographs began. The portrait on the left was taken on the third night — he was tired, it was hot, and for a moment he forgot to perform for the lens.

"The best night market photographs are not about what is for sale. They are about the people who stay when everything closes."

04 · Textile stall under fluorescent light, Chatuchak weekend market

Colour becomes a different language at night. Under fluorescent light, reds turn electric. Blues disappear into black. The photograph above is almost entirely magenta and green — colours that would look artificial in daylight but feel natural at 10 PM under a corrugated roof.

05 · A quiet moment between rushes, 11:15 PM

The quietest moment at a night market is just before closing. The crowd thins, the vendors begin packing, and the temporary architecture of stalls and tarpaulins starts coming down. This is when the market reveals its second self — not as a place of commerce, but as a place of work. The photographs from this hour are always the best.

06 · Last customer, Talad Rot Fai, 12:07 AM

I packed my camera at midnight and walked home through streets that smelled of grilled meat and diesel. The photographs felt like evidence — not of anything extraordinary, but of ordinary life, well-lived and well-lit.

· · ·

All photographs shot on a Fujifilm X-T5 with a 35mm f/1.4 lens. No flash. No tripod. Just patience.
